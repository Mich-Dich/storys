
# World building (The Artificer of the Deep)

## A Story Framework
John Doe is seventeen years old, with short black hair and eyes that have learned to read darkness better than faces. He crossed over from Earth three years ago—not dramatically, not with fanfare, but with the quiet dislocation of a door that should not have been there becoming a door that was.

Before the crossing, he was a lead engineer at Rheinmetall. German automotive and arms manufacturing. Precision. Systems. The beautiful logic of cause and effect. He was 27 years old and brilliant. The kind of mind that looked at a combustion engine and saw not just pistons and fuel but the elegant dance of controlled explosions, the mathematics of thermal expansion, the metallurgy of stress tolerances.

That mind did not stop working just because the world changed.

## The Loner's Calculus
John does not share his findings. This is not greed. Greed implies a desire for possession, for exclusivity as a form of status. John feels nothing of the sort. He does not share because he knows.

He has seen what happens when powerful technology is released without the corresponding social infrastructure to absorb it. He has read the history of gunpowder in Europe, of nuclear fission in his own world, of the internal combustion engine and what it did to the atmosphere and the geopolitical landscape. He knows that a discovery is never just a discovery—it is a lever, and someone will always want to move the world with it.

The magic of this world is already dangerous. The people who wield it are, by and large, trained in application, not understanding. They learn spells like children learn nursery rhymes—memorized, repeated, effective, but hollow at the core. Give them tools they do not understand, and they will break the world with them. Not from malice. From ignorance.

So John invents alone.

He does not feel lonely. Loneliness is a hunger for connection, and John's hungers are directed elsewhere—at the geometry of magicules, at the slow unraveling of a dungeon's secrets, at the precise angle of a spiral pattern and what it does to heat differentials. The city above has people who would call him friend if he let them. He does not let them.

He is not cruel. He is not cold. He is simply... occupied.

## The Dungeon as Home
Most adventurers treat dungeons as resources. You enter, you kill, you collect, you leave. The air is thick with magicules, and prolonged exposure leaves behind a *residue*—an invisible, viscous buildup that clings to flesh, organs, and the channels through which magic flows.

On the surface, the human body can clear this residue naturally. Slowly, over hours or days, the residue breaks down and dissipates. A good night's sleep in fresh air is usually enough after a shallow delve.

But inside a dungeon, the magicule concentration is too high. The body's natural clearance cannot keep up. Residue accumulates faster than it can be shed. Sickness follows. Confusion. Hallucinations. Muscle spasms. Push further, and you risk permanent mutation, internal crystallization, madness, death.

The guilds enforce strict limits based on rank—G through SSS—and the higher your rank, the longer your body's natural tolerance allows you to stay. But even SSS-rank delvers eventually need to surface. They need days of clean air, regular purges, and sleep in a bed that does not hum with ambient power.

John lives in the dungeon.

Not in the shallow tunnels near the entrance, where the light still filters through and the magicule concentration is barely elevated. He lives *deep*. Not because he is reckless, but because he has solved a problem that everyone else has accepted as unsolvable.

The high magicule concentration serves him. It accelerates his recovery during experiments. It feeds his crystal growth process. It is, in a very real sense, *fuel*.

He has learned to purge—not as a crude, external dialysis, but as an internal, continuous process that *augments* his body's natural clearance. He sheds residue faster than it can form, even in the deepest tunnels.

### The Purge Weave
The **Wave** pattern, when applied to the human body, creates a rhythmic pulse that loosens the bonds of accumulated residue. The **Net** pattern, when woven through the Wave, provides structure—a sieve that catches the loosened residue and channels it out.

John has integrated these patterns into his very being.

**Unconscious venting:** A near-invisible Wave plays constantly across his skin, like a heat sink bleeding off thermal energy. It works alongside his body's natural clearance, accelerating it. He does not think about it. It is as automatic as his heartbeat, developed through months of practice while he slept, while he ate, while he walked through dark tunnels.

**Active deep purge:** When he has pushed himself—after a major fight, after a long crafting session—he performs a focused Net+Wave weave *internally*. He sweeps his own body like a room, collecting residue from his organs, his bones, his magical channels—places the unconscious venting cannot reach. It is painful. It is exhausting. It resets his internal residue to zero, something no surface-born natural clearance could ever achieve.

Why don't others do this?

*Skill barrier:* Most mages learn spells, not primitives. They cannot weave a Net and a Wave together because they have never truly understood what a Net *is*. The precision required to weave internally, without incinerating your own organs, is beyond all but the most obsessive.

*Mental barrier:* The process requires touching your own soul with your mind. Most people find this horrifying. John finds it... interesting. He has always been comfortable inside his own head.

*The Dissonance:* The dungeon is alien. Prolonged exposure creates a psychological wrongness—a sense of being watched, of losing self, of the stone walls pressing in. This dissonance *slows* natural residue clearance for most people, as if the dungeon's very presence interferes with the body's healing. John, a loner who has never quite fit anywhere, experiences less of this. The dungeon's silence is not hostile to him. It is simply *quiet*. He prefers quiet. And because his dissonance is lower, his natural clearance remains more effective—giving his purge weaves an even greater advantage.

## Trading in Shadows
The city does not know John Doe lives in the dungeon.

Officially, he is missing. A seventeen-year-old who entered the Deep three years ago and never came out. There was a search, briefly. His name is on a list somewhere, marked with the cold finality of presumed death.

Unofficially, he trades through channels that ignore regulations.

There are always people willing to buy high-quality dungeon materials without asking questions. Fences. Black market alchemists. Guild officials with flexible ethics and heavy gambling debts. John has learned to find them. He sells crystals—the natural ones, never his artificial creations—and monster parts, carefully butchered and preserved. He buys essential equipment: food that does not spoil, replacement tools, the occasional book.

He does not buy much. He needs little.

His real wealth is not in coin. It is in knowledge.

## The Obsession
John is obsessed with two things: magic and dungeons.

He spends most of his time inside, living in a cavern he has claimed as his workshop. It is deep enough that the ambient magicule concentration is high, but not so deep that monsters wander through constantly. He has reinforced the entrances with simple traps and alarms—nothing magical, just string and bells and strategically placed stones. Sometimes the simple solutions are best. The internal space is about 20m by 50m with veriable sealing hight from 3m to 10m. John build his workshop by first leveling the floor (filling the 1 corner that dips down by creating a floor with wood), then adding some furniture like 2 tables, 2 chairs, a bed all created from resources in the dungeon or bougth at the surface and transported down. He has multiple fight sources like torches, glowing stones and lumines moss.

He studies patterns the way his Earth colleagues studied differential equations. He breaks them down into their components, tests them under controlled conditions, maps their behaviors. He has filled notebooks—waterproofed, monster-leather-bound notebooks—with diagrams and observations.

*Line + Spiral = heat with directional push.*
*Net + Wave = vibration with structural integrity.*
*Point + Chevron + Fork = split focus with threshold amplification.*

He is building a unified theory of magic. He is not there yet. But he is closer than anyone in this world has ever been.

And he has begun to *grow*.

## The Crystal Problem
It is widely known that certain materials can bend the stream of magicules. Dungeon crystals are the most common example. They grow naturally, forming intricate lattices filled with *hundreds* of internal lines—some straight, some curved, some spiraling, some branching. These lines are not isolated patterns but overlapping, intersecting geometries that collectively determine how magicules flow through the crystal.

A natural crystal is rarely useful. Most contain a chaotic jumble of lines that cancel each other out or produce no meaningful effect. But occasionally, by chance, a certain *number* of those lines will align—enough spirals clustered together, enough straight lines running parallel—to create a coherent function. A heating stone, for example, might contain two hundred spiral lines interwoven with fifty anchors. The spirals generate heat; the anchors keep it from dissipating too quickly. The result is a stone that warms a room when magicules pass through.

These crystals are valuable because they allow normal people to access magical effects without studying complex patterns. A heating stone can warm a room. A kinetic crystal can power a simple trap. But the effect is always crude, never optimized, limited by the random hand of nature.

The deeper problem is that natural crystals cannot be modified. Their internal patterns are set during growth, and no one has ever found a way to etch new lines into a finished crystal. The lattice is too dense, the structure too rigid. You cannot carve a spiral into something that resists carving at the molecular level.

John has not *solved* this problem. Not completely. But he has found a *workaround*.

## John's Growth Method (Imperfect, but Functional)
John grows his own crystals. But his method is nothing like the precise, layer-by-layer engineering he dreams of. He cannot yet control the lattice with atomic precision. He cannot build patterns line by line, plane by plane.

Instead, he does something far stranger.

He *casts* the desired pattern continuously—not at full power, but as a sustained, low-intensity weave. He holds the shape of the spell in the magicules around him, maintaining it for hours and days. Then he introduces a growth medium and a seed crystal. As the crystal forms, it grows *around* his maintained magicule field. The lattice solidifies, but where his magicules flow, the crystal leaves behind *hollows*—microscopic channels, empty spaces shaped exactly like the pattern he was holding.

When the growth is complete, the crystal contains not solid geometric lines but *voids*. Channels carved by the persistent presence of his magic. And those voids, when filled with a fresh flow of magicules, guide them exactly as a solid pattern would.

It is crude. It is inefficient. It is far from the theoretical limit.

But it *works*.

### The Cost
The process demands extreme ambient magicule concentration—more than exists anywhere except deep within a dungeon. The growth medium must be carefully prepared. The seed crystal must be pure, with minimal internal flaws.

And then there is the *attention*.

For three full days, John must maintain the spell. Not at combat intensity—he uses far fewer magicules than a real cast would require—but with *perfect consistency*. The pattern cannot waver. The flow cannot fluctuate. Any deviation, and the hollows will form wrong, or not at all.

Three days without sleep. Without eating more than what he can chew while holding the weave. Without lowering his concentration for more than a few seconds at a time.

It is exhausting. It is dangerous. A single mistake, and the crystal is worthless—or worse, it shatters, releasing the stored magicule tension in a sharp, concussive burst.

But when it works, John holds a crystal that contains *his* pattern. Imperfect. Inefficient. Far from what it could be.

But his.

## The Dungeon System
The dungeons are called **"Labyrinths"** or, more colloquially, **"the Deep."**

### Origins & Nature
- **Common Theory:** Most scholars believe Labyrinths are the corpse of a primordial, world-sized entity, its flesh turned to stone, its veins becoming tunnels, and its lingering life-force manifesting as monsters and magic. This explains why they "breathe" (magicules ebb and flow) and why they "bleed" (rare liquid ores).

- **John's Theory:** He believes they are not corpses, but *seeds*. A Labyrinth is a recursive, self-complicating spell-formula slowly "growing" into the world, with each monster and crystal being part of its arcane structure. This is a heretical, fringe theory. It fuels his obsession with understanding its patterns.

### Ecology & Resources

- **Monsters:** Magical fauna. Not "evil" but territorial. Butchering yields meat and valuable alchemical reagents. Primary income source for delvers.

- **Crystals:** The Labyrinth's "sap." Grow in clusters. Different types have different values:
    - **Aether Crystals:** Common. Used in basic enchantments and as magical batteries.
    - **Pattern Crystals:** Rare. Form in areas of high magical activity with complex, natural patterns frozen within. Can be studied to learn new patterns. John is obsessed with finding these—and eventually growing his own. A crystal with a pre-formed pattern allows near-instant spellcasting: pump magicules in, and the internal channels guide them automatically. Cast time drops from seconds to milliseconds. Mental load vanishes.

- **Herbs:** Bioluminescent fungi (**Wisp Caps**), mosses that grow only on certain stalactites (**Veil Moss**), pale heatless flowers that bloom in darkness (**Corpse Lilies**). Essential for alchemy and potions.

## The Magic System
### 1. The Core Process: Magicules and Patterns

- **Magicules:** Ambient, invisible particles existing in all things but highly concentrated in the Deep. A living body passively absorbs them. Casting is the act of expelling them with intent.

- **Patterns (The Primitives):** When magicules move in the shape of a pattern, they create a physical effect. These are the fundamental "letters" of magic. Most mages learn "spells"—complex, pre-made "sentences." Beginners use pre-made spells; only at a certain level do they attempt to learn patterns or create their own spells. John is learning patterns *first*, understanding magic from a different perspective.

### 2. Spellcasting vs. Weaving

- **Spellcasting (The Common Way):** A mage learns a specific sequence of patterns (e.g., Spiral + Point + Spiral = Fireball). Executed as a single, memorized action. Reliable, fast, inflexible.

- **Pattern Weaving (John's Way):** Combining primitives on the fly. Far more versatile. Requires immense concentration, mental imagery, and intuitive understanding of pattern interactions. Slower than spellcasting—unless you have years of practice. When fighting John still uses his own pre-made spells at times for the speed and predictability.

## Patterns Primitives (Complete List)
The fundamental components needed to create physical effects with magicules.

| Pattern | Shape | I/O | Effect |
|---------|-------|-----|--------|
| **Line** | Straight, unbroken segment | One input, one output | **Kinetic Force.** Linear acceleration or directional push. |
| **Spiral** | Continuous curve winding around center | One input (outer), one output (center) | **Rotation and Heat.** Tighter spiral = more heat. |
| **Net** | Grid of interlocking lines forming closed cells | Multiple inputs, multiple or single output | **Structure and Binding.** Barriers, entanglements, reinforcement. |
| **Wave** | Sinuous, repeating curve (sine or sawtooth) | One input, one output | **Pulse and Disruption.** Concussive blasts, destabilization, vibration. |
| **Point** | Single dimensionless node | Multiple inputs to single output (or reverse) | **Focus and Threshold.** Catalyst, intensifier, charge storage, sensory probe. |
| **Arc** | Smooth open curve (quarter-circle) | One input, one output | **Deflection.** Curves projectiles, bends attacks, sweeping force. |
| **Chevron** | V-shape with sharp vertex | Two inputs, one output (or reverse) | **Concentration/Splitting.** Merges flows or splits single flow. |
| **Zigzag** | Line reversing at sharp angles (lightning bolt) | One input, one output | **Oscillation/Vibration.** Shatters brittle materials, sonic bursts, resonance. |
| **Spur** | Straight line with smaller branch rejoining later | One input, one output | **Latching.** Stuttering effect, clinging, delayed effect, repeating pulse. |
| **Lens** | Two arcs bulging outward, meeting at ends | One input, one output | **Focus/Expansion.** Narrower intense stream or wider diffuse field. |
| **Möbius** | Loop with half-twist, continuous edge | One input and output at same point | **Inversion.** Reverses polarity (heat↔cold, push↔pull, bind↔repel). |
| **Cage** | Grid of intersecting lines forming closed lattice | Multiple inputs, one output (center) | **Containment.** Static field, barriers, charge storage, trapping. |
| **Fork** | Single line splitting into three or more branches | One input, multiple outputs | **Divide.** Splits effect into weaker copies. Multi-target or area distribution. |
| **Spike** | Long narrow triangle with sharp apex | One input (base), one output (apex) | **Penetration.** Ultra-thin dense stream. Piercing force, ward-breaking. |
| **Anchor** | T-shape with crossbar and serrated base | One input (top), one output (bottom) | **Stabilization.** Prevents dissipation. Permanent wards, long-lasting traps. |
| **Spring** | Tightly wound coil (compression spring) | One input, one output | **Storage/Release.** Stores potential energy, releases with amplified force. |
| **Sieve** | Parallel lines with perpendicular bridges (grid with openings) | One input (face), multiple outputs | **Filtering.** Separates magicules by property. Purification, selective barriers. |
| **Star** | Central point with multiple radiating lines | One input (center), multiple outputs | **Omnidirectional Release.** Broadcasts evenly. Flash of light, shockwave, aura. |
| **Braid** | Three or more lines interweaving over-under | Multiple inputs, multiple outputs | **Combine.** Blends flows into complex effect. Plasma bolt (Spiral+Line). |
| **Hook** | Curved line ending in sharp backward barb | One input (straight end), one output (barb) | **Grapple/Reel.** Pulling force that latches. Difficult to break. |
| **Pinwheel** | Four curved blades meeting at central hub | One input (hub) or multiple outputs | **Rotation/Shear.** Converts linear to rotational. Cutting whirlwinds, slicing. |
| **Dome** | Hemisphere of intersecting arcs | Multiple inputs (rim), one output (apex) | **Envelopment.** Protective bubble. Deflects attacks from above. |
| **Ribbon** | Long twisting strip folding without crossing | One input, one output | **Torsion.** Twisting force along axis. Unscrewing, wringing, structural failure. |
| **Prong** | Two parallel lines connected by bridge at one end (tuning fork) | Two inputs (free ends), one output (bridge) | **Interference.** Cancellation, standing waves, resonant frequency. |
| **Scatter** | Dense branching network ending in tiny points (fractal tree) | One input (trunk), many outputs (tips) | **Diffusion.** Fine mist, wide-area weak effect. Clouds, subtle binding, slow poison. |
| **Lock** | Two interlocking offset U-shapes | Two inputs, two outputs | **Mutual Binding.** Flows inseparable. Link objects, tandem movement, magical handcuffs. |
