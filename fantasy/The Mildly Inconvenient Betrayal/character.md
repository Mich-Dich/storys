
## **John Doe (The MC)**

**Visual**
- Late teens/early 20s, medium height, lean but not athletic
- Messy dark hair that he clearly never styles
- Eyes that are either half-closed or looking at something slightly to the left of whoever's talking
- Worn, practical robes — nothing dramatic, no glowing runes
- Always looks like he just woke up from a nap he didn't mean to take

**Character (who he is at his core)**
- Fundamentally uninvested in anything requiring effort
- Genuinely likes being helpful, but only if help is easy
- Has never had a "calling" or a "destiny" — just a series of mild curiosities
- Somehow kind, but in a distant, "I hope your day goes fine" way
- Treats world-ending threats like traffic jams: annoying, but not worth getting worked up over

**Personality**
- Loose, relaxed, almost horizontal in energy
- Makes terrible jokes at terrible times because he's bored
- Takes absolutely nothing seriously, including his own survival
- Can make life-changing decisions on a whim — and they always work out, which he finds mildly interesting but not surprising
- Genuinely confused when people get emotional about things

**Behavior**
- Walks slowly even during emergencies
- Sighs a lot, but not dramatically — just tired of existing
- Stretches before doing anything impressive, as if casting a legendary spell requires the same warm-up as getting off a couch
- Forgets people's names immediately but remembers useless details (e.g., "You dropped your left glove during the goblin fight")
- When asked to do something, responds with "Yeah okay" in a tone that suggests he's agreeing to take out the trash

**Strengths**
- Absurdly, accidentally competent at anything he tries
- Mastered magic by accident — only wanted to automate daily chores
- Completely unshakable — doesn't panic, doesn't freeze, doesn't get emotionally compromised
- Reads situations with weirdly accurate intuition (but doesn't care enough to act on it strategically)
- Physically lazy but magically efficient — finds the shortest path between two points every time

**Flaws**
- Zero follow-through — loses interest immediately after solving a problem
- Cannot be bothered to explain his plans (partly because he doesn't have plans)
- Hates moving quickly so much he'll invent a spell to avoid jogging
- Emotionally useless — cannot comfort, cannot celebrate, cannot mourn properly
- His casual attitude makes everyone around him feel slightly insane

## **The Hero**

**Visual**
- Tall, broad-shouldered, square jaw that could cut glass
- Shining armor (polished daily by someone else)
- Flowing cape that defies physics by always catching wind indoors
- Handsome in a way that feels manufactured, like a statue that learned to speak
- Blue eyes that somehow twinkle during speeches

**Character**
- Genuinely good — not performative, but also not self-aware
- Believes destiny is real and he is its favorite child
- Has never doubted himself for more than 30 seconds
- Loves his friends deeply but struggles to see them as people instead of roles (The Healer, The Tank, etc.)

**Personality**
- Earnest to the point of exhaustion
- Speaks like every sentence is being carved into a monument
- Cannot detect sarcasm, irony, or disinterest
- Secretly lonely because no one talks to him like a normal person
- Gets genuinely moved by his own speeches

**Behavior**
- Poses before entering rooms
- Places a hand on people's shoulders during serious conversations
- Says things like "This is our moment" without irony
- Practices dramatic lines in mirrors when he thinks no one's watching
- Always volunteers for the hardest task, then looks surprised when it's hard

**Strengths**
- Inspires genuine loyalty through sincerity, not manipulation
- Physically powerful — trained his whole life
- Never gives up, which has saved the party more times than John will admit
- Excellent strategic mind, but only for noble tactics (sneaking feels wrong to him)

**Flaws**
- Cannot adapt to unexpected situations — his scripts have scripts
- Treats John's competence as a given, never worth acknowledging
- Dismisses practical solutions if they don't feel "heroic"
- His need for narrative satisfaction sometimes endangers everyone

## **Healer Girl**

**Visual**
- Soft features, round cheeks, warm brown eyes
- Wears comfortable white robes with practical pockets (she's seen things)
- Always has medical supplies visibly strapped to her body
- Hair pulled back but with strands falling loose — permanently in "busy but kind" mode
- Smiles easily, laughs quietly

**Character**
- The emotional core of the party, but not naive
- Became a healer because she hates watching people hurt
- Secretly exhausted by everyone's drama
- Notices everything about everyone — including how little John is appreciated
- Too polite to say what she really thinks

**Personality**
- Genuinely kind, not performatively nice
- Calls everyone soft nicknames ("sweetie," "dear," "hun") and means them
- Has a breaking point that no one has ever reached because she's terrifying when angry
- Finds John weirdly comforting — he's the only one who doesn't demand her emotional labor
- Quietly competent, quietly tired, quietly done with everyone's nonsense

**Behavior**
- Pats people's arms after healing them
- Carries snacks for everyone because no one else remembers to eat
- Rolls her eyes internally during heroic speeches
- Asks John "Are you okay?" and actually waits for an answer
- Mumbles complaints under her breath while smiling outwardly

**Strengths**
- Elite healer — can bring people back from near-death
- Emotionally intelligent — reads rooms instantly
- Keeps the party functional (reminds Tank to eat, patches Fencer's ego, etc.)
- The only person John occasionally listens to

**Flaws**
- Avoids conflict until it explodes
- Spreads herself too thin trying to take care of everyone
- Underestimates her own importance (thinks healing is "just support")
- Sometimes enables bad behavior by smoothing things over instead of confronting

## **Tank Guy**

**Visual**
- Massive — built like a wall that learned to walk
- Scarred face, broken nose, missing part of an ear (all worn like medals)
- Heavy, dented armor that he refuses to replace ("It's got character")
- Shaved head, thick neck, hands that could crush stone
- Moves like someone who's never been asked to be gentle

**Character**
- Genuinely good-hearted but deeply simple
- Loves fighting, eating, drinking, and his friends (in that order)
- Has never had an original thought and is fine with that
- Loyal to a fault — would die for the Hero without understanding why

**Personality**
- Loud, proud, and slightly oblivious
- Laughs at his own jokes, which are not good
- Calls John "buddy" in a way that's 10% friendly and 90% condescending (unintentionally)
- Gets the party's morale up through sheer enthusiasm
- Secretly insecure about his intelligence, so he overcompensates with volume

**Behavior**
- Slaps people on the back hard enough to stagger them
- Eats like he's competing against time
- Asks "Can I just hit it?" for every problem
- Celebrates every victory like he personally solved it (even when he didn't)
- Forgets John exists until he needs something identified or unlocked

**Strengths**
- Nearly unkillable — tanking is his only skill and he's perfected it
- Excellent battlefield instincts (fast, reactive, physical)
- Never hesitates — charges first, asks questions never
- Surprisingly good at intimidating people without realizing he's doing it

**Flaws**
- Dismisses anything he doesn't understand as "magic nerd stuff"
- Cannot read a room, a map, or a person's emotions
- Assumes physical strength equals overall value
- Loudly takes credit for group victories

## **Fencer Girl**

**Visual**
- Tall, lean, graceful — moves like water
- Sharp features, sharp eyes, sharp everything
- Wears fitted, elegant armor that prioritizes mobility over protection
- White hair (dyed or natural — no one dares ask) tied in a high ponytail
- Never looks disheveled, even after battle

**Character**
- Comes from nobility but refuses to admit it
- Obsessed with perfection in all forms (combat, appearance, social grace)
- Deeply lonely but masks it with coldness
- Respects skill above all else — which is why John confuses her
- Has never apologized for anything in her life

**Personality**
- Cool, elegant, admired, and completely emotionally unavailable
- Speaks in precise, measured sentences (unless she's angry)
- Treats kindness as weakness but secretly craves it
- Finds John's existence offensive — how dare someone that good care that little
- Competitive about everything, including things that aren't competitions

**Behavior**
- Flicks her ponytail when annoyed
- Practices sword forms alone at dawn (won't admit it)
- Stares at people until they look away
- Gives John backhanded compliments ("Your fireball was... effective")
- Never thanks anyone directly — the absence of criticism is her gratitude

**Strengths**
- Top-tier duelist — speed and precision over power
- Strategic mind for positioning and tactics
- Cultured, educated, socially capable (when she chooses to be)
- Extremely hard to surprise or fluster

**Flaws**
- Cannot express vulnerability — will die before asking for help
- Dismisses people who aren't "excellent" at something
- Secretly desperate for approval but would never admit it
- Underestimates John because he doesn't look or act like a professional

## **The Dark Lord**

**Visual**
- Tall (not naturally — magically enhanced posture), gaunt, ancient
- Dark robes that flow without wind (he just thinks it's cool)
- Pale skin, glowing eyes (red or purple — changes with mood)
- Crown of jagged shadows that he absolutely designed himself
- Long silver hair, because if you're going to be evil, commit to the aesthetic

**Character**
- Ancient, powerful, theatrical, and deeply lonely
- Became the Dark Lord because it seemed like a good career path
- Genuinely enjoys villainy but loves *narrative* more
- Has been planning this final battle for centuries
- Secretly terrified that no one will remember him properly

**Personality**
- Dramatic to the point of self-parody (but unaware of it)
- Speaks in eloquent monologues he's rehearsed for years
- Cynical about everything except the importance of storytelling
- Has a code of ethics for evil (doesn't kill messengers, doesn't kick puppies, etc.)
- Emotionally invested in how things are *supposed to go*

**Behavior**
- Pauses dramatically before every action
- Gestures broadly when speaking (practiced in mirrors)
- Sighs heavily when reality deviates from his expectations
- Complains to his minions about poor narrative pacing
- Keeps a journal where he rates his own evil speeches (mostly 7/10, needs work)

**Strengths**
- Overwhelming magical power — could destroy the world if he wanted
- Patient, methodical, and genuinely intelligent
- Surprisingly good manager of his evil empire (infrastructure matters)
- Self-aware enough to be funny, not self-aware enough to stop being dramatic

**Flaws**
- Paralysis when things don't follow the script — cannot improvise
- Desperately wants validation from heroes who will never give it
- His love of theater makes him predictable
- Cannot comprehend John's casual attitude — it breaks his brain
