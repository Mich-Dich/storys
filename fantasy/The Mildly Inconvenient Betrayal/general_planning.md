

## Title:   The Mildly Inconvenient Betrayal

---

## **Tone & Core Idea**

This is not just comedy. It’s **deadpan absurdity colliding with epic fantasy seriousness**.

The world believes it’s in a legendary, destiny-driven final battle.

John believes he’s in a mildly disappointing group project.

The humor comes from:

* Genre expectations vs. reality
* Emotional speeches vs. practical thinking
* Life-or-death stakes vs. “I’m a little bored”

---

## **Main Cast (refined dynamics)**

* **John Doe (MC)**
  22, versatile mage, technically indispensable.
  Emotionally? Checked out.
  His real problem: *he’s too competent to be appreciated properly.*

* **The Hero**
  Classic moral compass. Speaks like every sentence is being engraved into stone.

* **Healer Girl**
  Kind, supportive, calls everyone by soft nicknames. Thinks John is “reliable,” which is somehow worse than being ignored.

* **Tank Guy**
  Loud, proud, gets all the cheers. Calls John “buddy” in a way that feels condescending without meaning to.

* **Fencer Girl**
  Cool, elegant, admired. Treats John like background infrastructure.

* **The Dark Lord**
  Ancient, powerful, theatrical… and deeply invested in how this scene is *supposed* to go.

---

## **ACT 1 – The Setup (The World Takes Itself Seriously)**

We open in the **Dark Lord Throne Room**:

* 40-meter ceilings, banners, ominous lighting, probably lava somewhere for no reason.
* The Hero’s party enters like this is the final boss of existence.

Everything is *epic*:

* Echoing footsteps
* Cloaks dramatically swaying
* Weapons humming with power

The Hero steps forward and delivers a speech about:

* Hope
* Humanity
* Destiny

The Dark Lord responds with:

* Cynicism
* Power
* A rehearsed offer to defect

It’s very clear:
**Both of them have imagined this moment for years.**

---

## **ACT 2 – John’s Internal Breakdown (The Real Story Begins)**

While the titans of ideology clash verbally…

John is mentally elsewhere.

He’s running a quiet internal audit of his life:

* “I carry 80% of the utility.”
* “No one remembers my spell names.”
* “Last week I prevented a catastrophic mana collapse and Tank got applause for hitting something.”

He reviews the Dark Lord’s offer:

* Gold? Nice.
* Power? Interesting.
* Fear? …now we’re talking.

And here’s the key:
**He doesn’t feel betrayed, angry, or corrupted.**

He’s just… curious.

“What if I tried being the problem for once?”

That’s it. That’s the turning point. Not trauma. Not manipulation. Just vibes.

---

## **ACT 3 – The Worst-Timed Decision in History**

The Hero and Dark Lord are mid-argument about morality.

And John…

**starts walking.**

No announcement.
No dramatic aura.
Just footsteps echoing in a massive hall.

Everyone notices, but no one understands.

He crosses the battlefield like someone switching tables in a cafeteria.

---

## **ACT 4 – The Pledge (Socially Unacceptable Behavior)**

John stands next to the Dark Lord and gives his “pledge”:

* Half-hearted tone
* Constant disclaimers
* Basically a verbal contract negotiation

Examples of the vibe:

* “I’ll try this out.”
* “If the environment is toxic, I’m leaving.”
* “Do you offer flexible evil hours?”

The Dark Lord is visibly struggling because:
**this is not how betrayal speeches work.**

Still… technically, this counts.

So he accepts.

Reluctantly.

---

## **ACT 5 – Combat (The Joke Hits Hard)**

John turns around.

Raises his hand and with a wide open palm he aims directly at the hero. He tenses up for the coming attack.

John Fires a fireball. It shoots off to the left from the palm, almost 90 degree from where his hand is aiming.

It slams into part of the throne, narrowly missing something ancient and priceless.

The Dark Lord immediately snaps:

“DO YOU HAVE ANY IDEA—”

John:

“Relax, adjusting.”

Second shot.

Third shot.

Fourth shot.

Perfect accuracy.

Efficient. Fast. Clinical.

The Hero’s party, built up as legends…

**goes down in seconds.**

No heroic comeback.
No emotional last stands.

Just:
*impact → silence → bodies hitting the floor*

The tone shift is brutal and hilarious.

---

## **ACT 6 – Emotional Vacuum**

The room goes quiet.

This was supposed to be:

* A long battle
* A symbolic victory
* A defining moment in history

Instead it feels like someone skipped to the end of the movie.

John looks at the Dark Lord and, completely flat:

“That was an impressive display of overwhelming evil dominance.”

Pause.

“Very efficient.”

It sounds like a performance review.

---

## **ACT 7 – Exit, Stage Left**

John stretches slightly, like he just finished a task.

“I’ll be taking that room over there.”

No permission requested.

He just… leaves.

---

## **ACT 8 – The Aftermath (Dark Lord’s Crisis)**

The Dark Lord stands alone in the massive hall.

Surrounded by:

* Fallen heroes
* Broken expectations
* A completely ruined narrative arc

He doesn’t celebrate.

He doesn’t laugh.

He just processes.

Slowly.

Painfully.

“…I had phases planned.”

A minion cautiously approaches:

“My lord… shall we pursue him?”

The Dark Lord, still staring at the doorway:

“No.”

Pause.

“I need to understand what just happened.”

---

## **Optional Continuation Hooks (because this can absolutely become a series)**

If you expand this beyond a one-shot:

* John treats villainy like a temporary internship
* The Dark Lord becomes weirdly dependent on John’s efficiency
* The world struggles to process a villain who is:

  * Polite
  * Honest
  * Mildly disengaged
  * Extremely dangerous

Meanwhile, John is just exploring:

“Fear is a refreshing change of pace.”

---

## **Why This Works (since you accidentally made something smart)**

* It **subverts fantasy tropes** without needing parody references
* The comedy is **character-driven**, not just random
* John is funny because he’s **rational in an irrational genre**
* The Dark Lord becomes funny because he’s **emotionally invested in narrative structure**
