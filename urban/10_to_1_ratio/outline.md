The following arcs move from the shotgun marriage to the launch of the *Kitten*, with romance and character growth threaded throughout. John’s point of view remains central; all arcs are narrated with his dry, self-deprecating voice.

### Arc 1: The Gilded Cage — First Month of Marriage

**Goal:** Establish the absurdity of John’s new life, showcase Lilith’s world, and plant the seeds of boredom that will drive the plot.

* **Immediate aftermath:** John signs the contract under duress, still reeling. He’s taken to Lilith’s massive estate after he said goodby to his mother (confesses the story and his mother reveals that the room number was 606 not 909). Culture shock as he realises the scale of her wealth (manor, staff of dozens, private security).
* **John leans fully into his “demands”** out of spite, expecting Lilith to eventually break and negotiate boundaries. She never does. Everything he asked for arrives: the gaming room, the masseuse, breakfast in bed, the wall he can watch being repainted. He floats in the pool while maids gently steer him away from the edge so he doesn’t drown in his sleep.
* **Character moments:** John’s internal monologue wars between bliss and guilt. He genuinely hates work, but being a completely passive ornament feels… hollow. He misses purpose, not a job.
* **Lilith’s distance:** She’s cordial but rarely present, buried in business. John assumes she sees him as a pet. In truth, she’s observing him—relieved he’s exactly as advertised and not a wolf in sheep’s clothing, but also puzzled by his genuine lack of ambition. She begins to find him curious rather than just convenient.
* **First public appearance:** John is dragged to a mandatory quarterly gala. He rebels by wearing a loud Hawaiian shirt and cargo shorts beneath a blazer he sheds at the door. Lilith, expecting humiliation, is instead amused—his antics make her laugh for the first time in years, and she realises he’s not trying to sabotage her. He’s just… like this. Ice begins to thaw.
* **Arc turning point:** After a month of endless leisure, John realises he’s bored out of his skull. The desire to *do* something, to *create*, stirs for the first time since his corporate death. He doesn’t know what yet, only that he’s tired of drifting.

### Arc 2: The Secret Blueprints — John’s Sailing Obsession

**Goal:** John finds his passion, misunderstanding drives a wedge, and the boat reveal becomes the first major romantic catalyst.

* **Discovery:** While aimlessly browsing the mansion’s library (looking for a novel, not work), John comes across a book on maritime history. He learns that in this world, steam and motor propulsion caught on so completely that recreational sailing barely exists. Traditional sailing ships are a niche historical hobby, and modern sailboats are unknown. A lightbulb goes off.
* **John withdrawals:** He retreats to his room for days, sketching. Naval architecture becomes his hyperfixation. He remembers enough from his previous life’s idle reading (he was a desk drone who dreamed of the sea) to produce rough designs. The staff is bewildered; he stops ordering absurd pranks and barely eats.
* **The Misunderstanding:** Lilith’s head of security reports John’s suspicious behaviour—secretive, nocturnal trips to fetch supplies (pens, giant rolls of paper), refusing to let maids clean his room. They deduce he’s hiding a lover. Lilith, masking hurt with cold fury, decides to catch him in the act. She plans a “raid” for the night after he next leaves his room.
* **The Presentation:** John, oblivious, nervously asks Lilith to hear him out. In her office, surrounded by her security team mid-planning, he unrolls his blueprints and pitches the 42-metre twin-keel sailing yacht with every comfort and safety feature imaginable. He’s terrified, expecting mockery.
* **Climax of the arc:** Three of the 5 present staff members laugh. John, mortified, flees. Lilith goes to him, the shock gives way to awe as she studies the drawings scattered in his room. She dismisses the misunderstanding with gentle humour, and makes two decisions that shock him: she’ll fund the project entirely (even gave double), and the staff who laughed will be reprimanded. The line “I’ll provide 300 million for the first month and more later” cements the shift: John isn’t just a kept man, he’s suddenly the director of a multi-million-dollar personal project.
* **Relationship beat:** Lying on his bed afterward, covered in a blanket, John admits he was so scared she’d think he was a leech asking for more money. Lilith tells him, quietly, that she’d rather fund a thousand boats than have him hide things from her. It’s the first genuinely intimate moment between them—and John realises Lilith isn’t just tolerating him. She *respects* this part of him.
