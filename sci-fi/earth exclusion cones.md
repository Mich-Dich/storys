War between interstellar powers is common. In a galaxy this vast, conflict is less an exception and more a rhythm. Borders shift. Fleets clash. Species rise, falter, and rise again.
But what the Kryllathi Dominion did to the Veloran Collective lingers in the mind.
The Velorans had lost. Their final defensive ring collapsed. Their fleet lay broken. They transmitted surrender codes across all frequencies. They powered down their weapons. They opened channels marked humanitarian and civilian.
The Kryllathi did not accept.
They followed the retreating Velorans into deep space. They tracked evacuation convoys. They burned hospital ships drifting with shields lowered. They sterilized mining colonies. Civilian transports, heavy with children and noncombatants, vanished in silent white flashes.
The killing stops only when Galactic Council intervention fleets arrive in overwhelming force and physically interpose themselves between hunter and prey.
Now, half a standard year later, the Council still debates. Committees convene. Reports circulate. Words like measured response and proportional sanctions echo through the Grand Convocation Hall.
To some, it sounds like justice.
To others, it sounds like permission.
Humanity listens.
And humanity decides.
