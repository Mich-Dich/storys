
## John Doe — The Accidental Space Emperor Dad
**Role:** Main Character
**Species:** Genetically engineered human
**Apparent Age:** Early–mid 30s
**Actual Age:** Flexible (enhanced longevity likely)
**Public Identity:** Richest human in the universe, owner of multiple mega-corporations
**Private Identity:** Chill guy on eternal vacation who just wants his kid to smile
### Personality
* Calm, polite, unflappable
* Kind without being sentimental
* Socially oblivious, not emotionally cold
* Curious about everything
* Treats impossible things as routine
* Zero need to dominate or impress

He doesn’t flex. Reality just bends around him.
### Strengths
* Absurd intelligence (engineering, strategy, science)
* Practically unlimited resources
* Near-perfect emotional control
* Genuinely compassionate
* Decisive without being impulsive
### Weaknesses
* Poor understanding of normal human limits
* Difficulty recognizing romantic cues (Alice is dying inside)
* Can accidentally destabilize societies by “helping”
* Detached from consequences due to scale of power
### Motivations
* Lilith’s happiness
* Intellectual curiosity
* Maintaining peace with minimal fuss
* Avoiding boring corporate responsibilities
### Fears (rarely acknowledged)
* Failing someone who depends on him
* Being alone again
* Becoming someone who treats people as problems to solve
### Quirks / Habits
* Solves things immediately instead of discussing them
* Gives gifts that are wildly disproportionate
* Speaks plainly, no theatrics
* Treats cosmic events like weather
### Relationship Notes
* **Lilith:** Absolute soft spot, father figure
* **Alice:** Trusts her completely, doesn’t realize she’s in love with him
* **Humanity:** Fond but distant
### Comedy Function
**Overpowered solution machine in a world of normal problems**


## Alice Copper — The Woman Holding the Galaxy Together With Sheer Professionalism
**Role:** Deuteragonist / POV anchor
**Species:** Genetically engineered human
**Apparent Age:** Late 20s to early 30s
**Position:** Executive assistant / operational head of John’s empire
**Actual Job:** Running everything while pretending she isn’t
### Personality
Outward:
* Elegant, composed, hyper-competent
* Diplomatic and precise
* Appears emotionally controlled

Inward:
* Constant screaming
* Deeply loyal
* Romantic feelings she refuses to act on
* Anxiety about catastrophic consequences
### Strengths
* Organizational genius
* Political and social intelligence
* Crisis management
* Immense self-discipline
* Ability to translate John’s madness into workable policy
### Weaknesses
* Overworks herself
* Avoids personal vulnerability
* Fear of losing control
* Doesn’t believe she deserves happiness outside duty
### Motivations
* Protect John (from others and from himself)
* Keep the companies and human space stable
* Stay near him, even if only professionally
* Avoid being forced into ultimate authority
### Fears
* John not needing her
* Being promoted into total responsibility
* Emotional exposure
* That her life has become only work
### Quirks / Habits
* Internal monologue at DEFCON 1 while smiling politely
* Plans for worst-case scenarios nobody else imagined
* Drinks tea/coffee as a coping mechanism
* Fixes problems before anyone knows they exist
### Relationship Notes
* **John:** Loves him, respects him, occasionally wants to shake him
* **Lilith:** Softens around her, pseudo-maternal instincts
* **Everyone else:** Professional wall of steel
### Comedy Function
**Straight man to cosmic absurdity + hidden emotional core**


## Lilith — The Small Engine of Chaos and Warmth
**Role:** Heart of the story
**Species:** Alien (cat-like traits)
**Age:** Child (adjust to tone, ~6–10 equivalent)
**Appearance:** Mostly human, thinner build, cat ears and tail
### Personality
* Sweet, affectionate, curious
* Imaginative and impulsive
* Empathetic toward living things
* Innocently literal
* Trusting, especially of John

She isn’t manipulative. She just assumes problems can be fixed.
### Strengths
* Emotional intelligence beyond her years
* Disarming innocence
* Adaptability
* Ability to bring out the best in others
### Weaknesses
* Naive about danger and politics
* Easily saddened by suffering
* Separation anxiety (subtle, not melodramatic)
* Child logic applied to cosmic situations
### Motivations
* Stay with her new family
* Explore everything
* Help things she thinks are “sad” or “broken”
* Make John and Alice happy too
### Fears
* Being abandoned again
* Losing John
* Conflict between her caretakers
* Loud anger or rejection
### Quirks / Habits
* Asks huge questions casually
* Physical affection (hugging, climbing onto John, tail signals mood)
* Names things creatively or incorrectly
* Collects small objects or creatures
### Relationship Notes
* **John:** Father, hero, safe place
* **Alice:** Authority figure + comfort figure
* **Strangers:** Friendly until given reason not to be
### Comedy Function
**Tiny wish generator that triggers galaxy-scale responses**


## Group Dynamic (Important)
This trio works because each fills what the others lack:
* John → Power + protection
* Alice → Stability + realism
* Lilith → Emotion + purpose

Together they form something dangerously close to a functional family, which is frankly suspicious in fiction and therefore delightful.
