# Chapter 2 – **Building a Future**
## Scene 1 – Morning in the Garden
The chapter opens inside the habitat.
Artificial sunlight rises over the rolling fields.
The Lunari are beginning to settle.
Some are building temporary shelters near the river.
Others explore the forest areas.
Children run through the grass chasing the bird-like maintenance drones.
Lucy walks through the growing settlement.
There is laughter now.
Something she hasn’t heard from her people in years.
But one thing is already noticeable.
Jake is not there.
Lucy eventually finds him standing on a hill overlooking the landscape, drinking coffee while staring at a holographic projection of several nearby star systems.
He looks… thoughtful.


## Scene 2 – The Practical Problem
Jake explains the obvious problem.
He promised usable ship, but his current material reserves are insufitent for some top of the line explored ships. Some thing that can keep them save and advance their own systems while in deep space for the next few thousend years. I want tou to live a good live exploring averathing that the universe has to offer.
Lucy is stunned, "That what you call usable??", she lightly protests about realisum, her livespan,  ...
Jake looks confused, while on this ship the medical system will halt your aging
with current systems he can live for a few thousend years more tells LYRA, and jake continues, and with advances in technology in those thousend years that time will expand, so maybe im immortal?
439 refugees hopes for simple survival, and now there is a human that offers them funtional imortallity because he thinks they are cute.
Jake is talking about **building a fleet**.
LYRA calmly lists the requirements:
* billions of tons of metals, listing around 90% of the periodic table
* rare superconductors
* exotic elements
* antimatter containment components
Jake just nods.
“Well. Guess we better go mining.”


## Scene 3 – The First Mining Operation
The ship arrives in a nearby asteroid belt.
Jake watches through the bridge viewport as thousands of asteroids drift slowly through space.
Lucy stands beside him.
She assumes mining drones will be used.
Jake shakes his head.
“Nah.”
He presses a control.
The ship fires a massive **mining beam**.
A glowing orange cutting laser slices through an asteroid the size of a city block.
The rock slowly splits apart.
Then the tractor systems activate.
The pieces are gently pulled toward the ship.
Lucy watches in disbelief.
“...That asteroid was two kilometers wide.”
Jake shrugs.
“Good starter rock.”


## Scene 4 – The Moon Walk
Jake decides to inspect a larger deposit on a nearby moon.
Instead of sending drones, he casually suggests going outside.
Lucy thinks he is joking.
He isn’t.
The airlock opens.
Outside, a **tractor field bubble** forms around them.
A perfectly transparent sphere of atmosphere held together by tractor beams.
They step onto the grey surface of the moon while still breathing normally.
Lucy stares in amazement.
Jake casually kicks a small rock and watches it bounce in low gravity.
“See? Field bubble.”
Lucy slowly turns in place, seeing the stars above them.
“…You built portable atmosphere.”
Jake nods.
“Very useful for picnics.”


## Scene 5 – Mining the Moon
Jake points toward a mountain ridge.
“Good metal veins there.”
Lucy watches as the ship above them fires multiple mining beams.
Entire sections of the moon are sliced away.
Massive chunks of metal-rich rock rise slowly into space as tractor beams capture them.
The scale is absurd.
Lucy realizes Jake could dismantle **entire celestial bodies** if he wanted.
Jake happily collects the materials.


## Scene 6 – The Unintentional Confession
Later inside the ship.
Lucy walks through a corridor and overhears Jake talking to LYRA.
Jake sounds slightly distracted.
“I mean… they could stay, you know.”
Lucy pauses.
Jake continues.
“The Garden’s big enough. The ship’s big enough.”
He sighs.
“…It would be nice.”
LYRA responds calmly.
“I calculate a 93% probability that you would enjoy their continued presence.”
Jake grumbles.
“Don’t say it like that.”
Lucy quietly walks away before being noticed.
Her tail moves slowly as she processes what she just heard.


## Scene 7 – LYRA Interferes
Later Lucy is in the Garden again.
LYRA speaks to her directly through nearby speakers.
“Lucy.”
Lucy looks up.
“Yes?”
LYRA says calmly:
“The captain is too embarrassed to express his preferences directly.”
Lucy blinks.
“…Preferences?”
“He would like the Lunari to remain with him.”
Lucy immediately blushes.
“W-with him?”
LYRA answers in her usual calm tone.
“Primarily you.”
Lucy turns completely red.


## Scene 8 – The Oppressor Planet
LYRA detects a nearby inhabited world.
It belongs to the same species that enslaved the Lunari.
Lucy is shocked.
Jake’s reaction is simpler.
“Oh. Perfect.”
Lucy becomes nervous.
“What are you going to do?”
Jake smiles.
“Mining.”


## Scene 9 – Descent
Jake’s ship enters the system.
Planetary defenses activate.
Missile platforms fire.
Orbital cannons engage.
Fighter squadrons scramble.
Jake ignores everything.
The ship simply continues descending through the atmosphere like an unstoppable mountain.
Missiles explode harmlessly against the shields.
Laser cannons bend around distortion fields.
Lucy watches the battle in stunned silence.
Jake casually drinks coffee.
“Bit rude of them.”

In the ocean. when the ship hovers above the deepest point in their ocean, the crew can go swimming. Lucy in shok knows exaktly what comes next. the luinari playing in the water while missels and railgun shots are fired at them.


## Scene 10 – Planetary Mining
Jake lands in a remote mountain region.
The ship hovers above the surface.
A massive mining beam fires downward.
The beam drills **tens of kilometers** into the planet’s crust.
Deep underground exotic metals are extracted and lifted into orbit.
Planetary defenses continue firing.
Jake simply finishes mining.
“Alright, that should do it.”
Then he leaves.


## Scene 11 – Ship Construction
Back in orbit near a quiet system.
Massive shipyards deploy from Jake’s vessel.
Automated fabrication systems begin assembling **new Lunari ships**.
Lucy watches from the observation deck.
Hull segments kilometers long float in formation.
Engines assemble themselves.
Weapons, shields, habitats.
Jake is essentially building **a small civilization fleet**.
Lucy realizes he truly intends to give her people a future.


## Scene 12 – The Quiet Moment (End of Chapter)
Late evening in the Garden.
Artificial sunset paints the sky orange.
Lucy finds Jake sitting beside the river.
They watch the construction lights of the growing ships in orbit.
Lucy thanks him.
Jake shrugs awkwardly.
They sit quietly for a moment.
Lucy’s tail slowly brushes against his arm without her noticing.
Jake notices.
Neither of them move away.
Lucy looks up at the artificial stars.
She speaks softly.
“My people planned to search for our lost home world.”
Jake nods.
“That sounds like a good adventure.”
Lucy glances at him.
“…Would you come with us?”
Jake smiles slightly.
“I was hoping you’d ask.”
Lucy looks at him for a long moment.
And for the first time since the beginning of their journey…
The idea of **staying with him** begins to feel like home.
